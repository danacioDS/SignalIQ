## The Problem

In today's financial markets, information is no longer a competitive advantage. It is a massive distraction.

Every day, thousands of headlines shape market perception, creating narrative currents that often run completely counter to what is actually happening in the order books.

Traditional analysis is split into two incomplete halves:

- **Sentiment analysis** measures media euphoria but ignores whether that current has the economic force to move prices.
- **Technical analysis** coldly analyzes price trends but advances blindly without understanding the narrative context.

The true danger for an investor is not a down market. It is a market where the public narrative remains hyper-bullish while the actual price has already stopped climbing.

That is exactly where severe corrections are incubated and bubbles burst.

## The Tetlock Nuance (Expert-Level Explanation)

Here is what Paul Tetlock actually found in his seminal 2007 paper *"Giving Content to Investor Sentiment: The Role of Media in the Stock Market"*:

Tetlock did **not** find that high sentiment predicts an immediate reversal. He found something more subtle:

- **Extreme negative sentiment (high pessimism)** predicts downward price pressure in the immediate short term, followed by a reversal back to fundamentals.
- When it comes to **high optimism (euphoria)** , the market can stay irrational longer.

The "reversal" does not just happen because people are happy. It happens when the **hype keeps going but the price stops moving.**

> *"Tetlock quantified how media sentiment creates temporary price pressures that eventually revert. My system builds on this by identifying the exact moment that pressure detaches from price action."*

## The Narrative Divergence Index (NDI)

**NDI(t) = [ S_news(t) - μ_s ] / σ_s − [ M_price(t) - μ_m ] / σ_m**

| NDI Range | Regime | Interpretation |
|-----------|--------|----------------|
| NDI < -1.5 | 🔵 Silent Accumulation | Prices rising without narrative support. Often precedes sustained uptrends. |
| NDI ≈ 0 | ⚪ Aligned | Narrative-price alignment. Healthy, sustainable trend. |
| NDI > 1.5 | 🟡 Narrative Exhaustion | Optimism persists but momentum weakens. The story is running out of new buyers. |
| NDI >> 1.5 with flat prices | 🟠 Divergence Warning | Institutional money may be exiting while public enthusiasm remains elevated. |
| NDI extreme with falling prices | 🔴 Severe Divergence | Maximum-risk environment. Historically associated with abrupt corrections. |

**Note:** The full taxonomy includes five regimes. The Silent Accumulation regime (NDI < -1.5) is the inverse signal — a potential entry opportunity when prices move without narrative support.
